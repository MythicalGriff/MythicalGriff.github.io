VIRTUAL REALITY

What is it used for?

Well, a lot of things, but here are the main:

VR is used worldwide by many branches of military and by the general public, VR is able to produce a worldlike simulation that could help to train humans for differents jobs and talents. In the military this is used to train soldiers in combat and other procedures such as drills and tours of the bases, as of currently this isn’t the most widely used method but it’s growth is dramatically speeding up as VR becomes a more viable option for training. The more VRs growth progresses the more technology we gain to really push the boundaries in sense stimulation and realistic simulations and gaming. Gaming has been dramatically pushed into the future with this technology, and is open to more options of different genres and of versatility. The VR using your body as the tool to game allows you to make far more precise and detailed actions, of which generally affects the environment. The amount of immersion that the VR gives you allows for an overall greater experience for the user. Along with these humans have worked to improve it with new technology to such as Haptic force-feedback gloves and the Omni-directional treadmill. he Virtual Reality Headset has proven to be of help in alleviating trauma and/or helping calm anxiety and anxiety disorders. Exposure therapy is a method used to help cure PTSD, however, it can not help in all cases. Exposure therapy puts the patient into a situation that had caused trauma, but in such cases as war veterans obtaining their trauma from battlegrounds, we can’t do that as not to put the patient into danger. This is when VR comes in to help, by building a situation that puts the patient in no danger whatsoever yet is still realistic enough to help with the trauma caused by it.
